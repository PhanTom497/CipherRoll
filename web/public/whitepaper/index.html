01 ·

Abstract

CipherRoll is a full-stack confidential payroll system deployed on Arbitrum Sepolia, powered by the Fhenix Coprocessor for Fully Homomorphic Encryption (CoFHE). It enables organizations to execute complete payroll workflows — from workspace creation through treasury-backed employee payout and verifiable audit evidence — without exposing salary amounts, budget reserves, or aggregate treasury posture to the public ledger. Core financial values are stored and computed as encrypted euint128 ciphertext handles, with arithmetic performed directly over encrypted state via CoFHE coprocessor instructions. This document describes the system's cryptographic model, contract architecture, governance framework, privacy boundary, compliance design, and full-stack deployment.

CipherRoll does not claim to hide all on-chain activity. It makes a narrower and more useful claim: the financially sensitive core — salary amounts, budget state, committed payroll, and runway — remains encrypted at all times, while the product remains transparent about the public metadata that any EVM-native system necessarily emits.

02 ·

The Problem: On-Chain Payroll Transparency

Blockchain-native payroll is a natural evolution for organizations operating in Web3 — it enables programmable compensation, transparent vesting schedules, cross-border settlement without intermediaries, and on-chain audit trails. But deploying payroll to a transparent ledger introduces a fundamental tension: the properties that make a public chain trustworthy also make it a surveillance surface for compensation data.

💸
Salary Amounts Become Inferable

Anyone observing the chain can reconstruct compensation patterns by correlating deposit sizes, transfer events, and payout timing across wallet addresses.

🏦
Treasury Posture Is Visible by Default

Funding timing, reserve levels, and drawdown cadence are all public. Competitors, employees, and adversaries can infer organizational financial health at any time.

🔗
Payout Events Are Linkable

Employee claim and settlement transactions are on-chain, creating a permanent, linkable record of individual payout amounts and timing.

📋
Audit Workflows Over-Disclose

Traditional on-chain audit review surfaces reveal far more than the compliance objective requires, often exposing employee-level data to external reviewers.

Prior approaches have addressed this through off-chain payroll with on-chain settlement — but this defeats the programmability and auditability benefits of a blockchain-native system. CipherRoll's approach is to use Fully Homomorphic Encryption at the contract level, keeping sensitive values encrypted throughout their entire lifecycle while preserving composability and supporting a complete, production-grade payroll workflow.

Solution Overview

CipherRoll combines five core capabilities into a unified, full-stack system deployable on any EVM chain with access to the Fhenix CoFHE coprocessor network.

🔐
Encrypted Payroll Core

Salary amounts, budget state, committed payroll, and runway are stored and computed as euint128 CoFHE handles. Arithmetic is performed over ciphertext — no plaintext ever reaches the host chain or contract operators.

💸
Real Treasury-Backed Settlement

Payroll runs reserve real treasury inventory and settle via live payout adapters — direct ERC20 transfer or a two-phase FHERC20-style wrapper-backed unshield flow that preserves confidentiality until finalization.

🛡️
M-of-N Governed Execution

Sensitive operations — payroll issuance, vesting, treasury configuration — require multi-party proposal approval before execution. Day-to-day operations remain single-admin for operational fluidity.

📦
Browser-Local Batch Payroll

CSV import, review before encryption, browser-side salary sealing, and retryable one-transaction-per-row submission. Salary plaintext never leaves the operator's device.

🧾
Aggregate-Only Audit Disclosure

Auditors see organization-level aggregates only — budget, runway, run counts, treasury status — via shared permit-backed decryption. Individual employee salary rows are structurally inaccessible.

📊
Tier A Compliance Exports

Aggregate compliance packages with explicit tax reserve basis-point policy, treasury posture, and on-chain receipt evidence — exportable as JSON or CSV without exposing employee salary data.

FHE Cryptographic Foundation

CipherRoll's privacy guarantees are grounded in the Fhenix CoFHE coprocessor — a system that executes FHE operations on behalf of smart contracts without requiring the host chain to implement FHE natively. This coprocessor model allows Solidity contracts to call FHE operations (FHE.add, FHE.sub, FHE.gte, FHE.select, FHE.asEuint128) and receive encrypted bytes32 ciphertext handles that can be stored and forwarded without any party learning the underlying value.

// Conceptual encrypted budget arithmetic — actual values remain ciphertext euint128 newBudget = FHE.add(_encryptedBudget[orgId], encryptedAmount); FHE.allowThis(newBudget); // permit future contract operations FHE.allow(newBudget, adminAddr); // permit admin decryptForView _encryptedBudget[orgId] = newBudget; // store handle, not plaintext

Decryption follows two paths: decryptForView (browser-local permit-backed plaintext reveal, never touching the host chain) and decryptForTx (on-chain proof submission for settlement finalization, which makes the decrypted value public as an explicit, auditable disclosure event).

Treasury Settlement Paths

Direct Settlement

Upon a verified employee claim, the DirectSettlementAdapter executes an immediate ERC20 token transfer to the employee wallet.

Wrapper-Backed Settlement

The WrapperSettlementAdapter shields ERC20 tokens into a ConfidentialPayrollToken (FHERC20-style) at funding time.

PhaseFunctionEffect
Phase 1 — Request requestPayrollSettlement Creates a pending unshield request stored on-chain. Confidential balance remains encrypted.
Phase 2 — Finalize finalizePayrollSettlement Employee submits a decryptForTx proof. Adapter calls claimUnshielded; underlying ERC20 is released. Amount becomes public — explicit disclosure event.

Route-Pinning Safety: Wrapper settlement requests are pinned to the adapter that created them. If the organization's treasury route is changed between phases, the finalize transaction reverts with "CipherRoll: settlement route changed".

Privacy Model & Boundary Analysis

CipherRoll's privacy model is deliberately precise. Rather than overstating what is hidden, the system provides a formal boundary between what remains encrypted and what is public.

ENCRYPTED Kept private on-chain as euint128
  • Organization budget (permit-backed admin decrypt only)
  • Committed payroll aggregate (permit-backed admin decrypt only)
  • Available runway (permit-backed admin decrypt only)
  • Individual employee salary allocations (employee-only decrypt)
  • Auditor aggregate handles (shared permit decrypt only)
  • Wrapper-backed confidential balances (until finalize proof)
PUBLIC Visible by EVM design or explicit emission
  • Transaction sender, recipient, nonce, gas, block, timestamp
  • Contract calldata for all write functions
  • Event logs and indexed topics
  • ERC20 transfer events and payout balances
  • orgId, admin address, treasury adapter, route id
  • paymentId, employee address, memo hash, vesting flags
  • Run status, funding deadline, allocation and claimed counts
  • Wrapper settlement amount after decryptForTx finalization

Identifier Leakage Mitigation

IdentifierRiskMitigation
Workspace / org idsMnemonic labels are guessableHigh-entropy label generator available; mnemonic use is operator choice
Treasury route idsReadable labels expose adapter intentHigh-entropy alternatives provided
Payment idsSequential ids allow enumerationNow generated with high-entropy helpers (Wave 3 hardening)

Design principle: CipherRoll's value is not "hide everything." Its value is "keep the financially sensitive core encrypted while staying honest about the public edges." This framing makes the system's guarantees verifiable rather than aspirational.

Permit Sharing Flow

Auditor access is mediated by a shared permit payload generated by the admin portal. The permit grants access only to the three aggregate handles (budget, committed, available) — not to any employee allocation handle.

Batch Payroll & Browser-Local Sealing

CipherRoll's batch payroll system enables operators to issue payroll for multiple employees from a CSV file without salary plaintext ever leaving the operator's device or reaching the backend.

Processing Pipeline

StepLocationAction
1. CSV ImportBrowserParse with encoding detection (UTF-8, UTF-16 LE/BE, BOM); column auto-detection
2. Row ValidationBrowserEmployee address checks, role assignment, base salary mapping
3. Review GateBrowserOperator sees all rows in plaintext before any encryption begins
4. Salary SealingBrowserencryptUint128 per row; plaintext removed from visible state immediately after
5. SubmissionBrowser → ChainOne wallet transaction per employee row; confirmed rows skipped on retry
6. Manifest StorageBackendorg id, run id, employee address, role slug, payment id, tx hash — never salary amounts

Scope Constraint: Batch payroll v1 is available in non-governed batch workspaces only. Governed workspaces display an explicit warning and cannot seal or submit batch rows.

Roadmap & Scope Boundaries

Development Wave Progression

Wave 1 · Protocol Core
Encrypted Payroll State & Client-Side Decrypt

Established the FHE payroll contract with encrypted budget arithmetic, homomorphic budgeting, wallet auth, and client-side decryptForView. Proved that a complete payroll workflow could be built over encrypted ciphertext handles without any plaintext exposure.

Wave 2 · Settlement + Auditor
Real Treasury-Backed Payout & Selective Disclosure

Added CoFHE SDK protocol alignment, real treasury-backed settlement adapters (direct and wrapper-backed), auditor selective disclosure architecture, and verifiable on-chain audit receipts with verify/publish modes.

Wave 3 · Submission Hardening
Proof Verification & Privacy Framing

Hardened proof verification, introduced truthful privacy boundary documentation, reduced identifier leakage with high-entropy payment id helpers, and shipped CipherBot v1 as a retrieval-backed product assistant.

Wave 4 · Backend Platform
Indexed Read Models & Shared SDK

Built the full backend with indexed chain data, a shared TypeScript SDK preventing frontend-backend drift, reporting and analytics APIs, notification generation, export formats, and an upgraded retrieval-backed CipherBot.

Wave 5 · Governance + Compliance
M-of-N Governance, Batch Payroll & Tier A Compliance

Implemented dual-mode M-of-N governance, browser-local batch payroll with sealed salaries, treasury exposure reporting with route health and payout backlog, and Tier A aggregate compliance packages with JSON/CSV export.

Intentionally Deferred

The following capabilities are acknowledged and intentionally out of scope for this submission: full tax automation and real authority integrations; multi-jurisdiction tax modeling; multi-network rollout beyond Arbitrum Sepolia; enterprise auth and role server model; and action-taking AI assistants.

16 ·

Conclusion

CipherRoll demonstrates that production-grade confidential payroll is achievable on a public EVM chain today. By grounding privacy in Fully Homomorphic Encryption at the contract level — rather than in off-chain compute, trusted execution environments, or zero-knowledge proving systems that require separate circuits — CipherRoll achieves a tight and verifiable privacy boundary: sensitive financial values stay encrypted throughout their lifecycle, while the system remains composable, auditable, and honest about the metadata that any EVM-native system necessarily makes public.

The five-wave build progression — from core FHE payroll state through settlement adapters, auditor disclosure, governance, batch payroll, and compliance exports — demonstrates that this architecture can support a full enterprise payroll workflow without sacrificing privacy guarantees at any stage.

The principal contribution of CipherRoll is not any single cryptographic primitive. It is the demonstration that FHE-encrypted state, real treasury settlement, multi-party governance, aggregate-only audit disclosure, and compliance reporting can be composed into a coherent, deployable, full-stack system with a truthful and formally documented privacy boundary.
